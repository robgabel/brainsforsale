# BrainsForSale — Brand Style Guide

---

## 1. Brand Essence

### What We Are

BrainsForSale is a knowledge infrastructure company. We package the world's best thinkers into portable, structured knowledge packs that people install into their AI tools. We sell ownership of expert thinking, not access to a chatbot.

### One-Line Positioning

**"The rare bookshop of AI."** Premium, curated, portable intelligence you own forever.

### The Core Inversion

Everyone else builds **destinations** — walled gardens where you chat with AI clones inside their platform. We build **infrastructure** — portable artifacts you own, load wherever you want, and combine however you like.

**Delphi monetizes access. We monetize ownership.**

### Brand Promise

Load a brain. Think better in seconds. Not someday — right now, in the tools you already use.

## 2. Brand Personality

### We Are

| Trait | What It Means in Practice |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Confident, not loud** | Our headlines are weight-300. Our product speaks for itself. We don't use exclamation marks in marketing. |
| **Precise, not clinical** | We use exact numbers ("284 atoms, 430 connections") instead of vague claims ("thousands of insights"). |
| **Intellectual, not academic** | We're for practitioners, not professors. Frameworks are for using, not citing. |
| **Warm, not soft** | We care about the people who build things. But we don't coddle. The product is rigorous. |

| **Builder-first, not consumer-first** | We respect our audience's technical fluency. Show the JSON. Show the install command. Don't hide the machinery. |

### We Are NOT

| Trait | Why We Avoid It |
|-------|----------------|
| Corporate | No stock photos of people shaking hands. No "enterprise-grade solutions." |
| Hype-driven | No "revolutionary" or "game-changing." The demo is the proof. |
| Cute or playful | No mascots, no emoji-heavy copy, no "meet your new AI bestie." |
| Apologetic | We don't preface things with "we're just getting started" or "it's not perfect yet." |
| Generic AI | We're not "AI-powered" — every product is AI-powered now. We're a knowledge product that happens to work with AI tools. |

## 3. Brand Voice

### Tone Spectrum

```
Casual ←——————————●——→ Formal
                  ↑
            "Smart friend who reads a lot"
```

We write like a sharp friend who just finished a great book and wants to tell you about it over coffee. Not a professor. Not a marketer. Not a robot.

### Voice Principles

**1. Lead with the demo, not the claim.**
Don't say we're good. Show an `/advise` output. The terminal screenshot is worth more than any tagline. "That demo IS the landing page."

**2. Use specifics, not superlatives.**
Replace "comprehensive" with "284 atoms from all 77 Implications editions." Replace "powerful" with "10 skills that chain into 4 workflows." Numbers are more persuasive than adjectives.

**3. Talk to builders, not buyers.**
Our audience is people who make things. They want to know how it works, not just what it does. Show the file structure. Show the JSON schema. Show the install command.

**4. Respect the thinker.**
Every brain represents a real person's intellectual output. We attribute everything. We use confidence scores. We say "Belsky would argue..." not "Belsky says..." because we're synthesizing, not quoting.

**5. Short sentences. Short paragraphs. White space.**
Our copy should feel like our design — measured, precise, breathing room between ideas.

### Writing Cadence

The BrainsForSale writing rhythm is: **short declarative sentence. Medium explanatory sentence. Then a payoff line that lands.**

> Knowledge graphs of the world's best thinkers, packaged as AI skills you actually use. Think better in seconds.

> Not a chatbot. Not a PDF. A skill pack that turns a knowledge graph into a thinking partner.

> One command. Any AI tool. Instant expertise.

## 4. Language Rules

### Words We Use

| Word/Phrase | When to Use |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Brain** / **brain pack** | The product. Always lowercase "brain" unless starting a sentence. |
| **Atoms** | The unit of knowledge. An atomic idea — one insight, one framework, one principle. |
| **Connections** | Typed relationships between atoms (supports, contradicts, extends, related). |
| **Skills** | The 10 interactive commands (/advise, /teach, /debate, etc.). |
| **Load** | How you install a brain. "Load a genius into your AI." Not "install" in marketing (though "install" is fine in docs). |
| **Thinker** | The person whose brain we've packaged. Not "thought leader," not "expert," not "influencer." |
| **Knowledge graph** | The structured network of atoms + connections. Use when talking to technical audience. |
| **Portable** | The brain works in Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT, Gemini — any AI tool. Not locked in. |
| **Think better** | The outcome we promise. Not "be smarter" (condescending) or "unlock insights" (generic). |
| **Structured thinking** | What we sell. Not summarized content — structured, connected, queryable thinking. |
| **Brain score** | Completeness metric. Transparency builds trust. |
| **Mashup** | Loading 2+ brains for cross-perspectives. The retention play. |
| **Brain fight** | Head-to-head comparison between two brains. Content format and skill. |
| **Claim your brain** | When the real thinker wants to verify/endorse their brain pack. |
| **Founding supporters** | First 100 buyers per brain. Not "early adopters" or "beta users." |

### Words We DON'T Use

| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **AI clone** / **digital twin** | Creepy, inaccurate. We're not cloning anyone. | "Brain pack" / "knowledge graph" |
| **Chatbot** | We are explicitly not a chatbot. This is the anti-positioning. | "Thinking skills" / "skill pack" |
| **Revolutionary** / **game-changing** | Empty hype. Our audience is allergic to it. | Show the demo. Let them decide. |
| **Unlock** | Overused SaaS verb. Meaningless. | "Load" / "get" / "install" |
| **Leverage** | Corporate jargon. | "Use" / "apply" |
| **Insights** (as a mass noun) | Vague. Every AI product promises "insights." | "Atoms" / "frameworks" / "connections" |
| **Content** (describing our product) | We don't sell content. We sell structured knowledge. | "Knowledge" / "thinking" / "atoms" |
| **Smart** / **intelligent** (describing the product) | Self-praise. Let the output speak. | Show a demo instead. |
| **Personalized** | Every AI product says this. | "Adapted to your context" / "applied to your situation" |
| **Best-in-class** / **world-class** | Generic superlatives. | Cite the specific number or quality marker. |
| **Solution** | The most meaningless word in B2B marketing. | Name the actual thing. "Brain pack." "Skill." "Knowledge graph." |
| **Empower** | Corporate buzzword. Patronizing. | "Give you" / "let you" |
| **Synergy** | Obviously. | Nothing. Delete the sentence. |
| **Thought leader** | Cringe. Even thought leaders hate this term. | "Thinker" / use their name. |
| **Influencer** | Wrong frame. We package intellectuals, not influencers. | "Thinker" / "author" / "researcher" |
| **Curated** (alone) | Overused. What ISN'T "curated" now? | "Curated from 77 editions" — always attach specifics. |
| **End-to-end** | Enterprise speak. | Describe the actual scope. |
| **Seamless** | Means nothing. | "One command" / "works in under 60 seconds" |

### Headline Patterns

**Pattern 1: Declarative. (Our default.)**
- "Load a genius into your AI."
- "Brains for sale."
- "10 thinking skills per brain."
- "Simple. Per brain."

**Pattern 2: Not X. Not Y. Z.**
- "Not a chatbot. Not a PDF. A skill pack."
- "Not summaries. Not quotes. Structured thinking."

**Pattern 3: Specific number + benefit.**
- "284 atoms. 430 connections. 10 skills."
- "One command. Any AI tool. Instant expertise."

**Pattern 4: Provocative question.**
- "What if you could just... buy the brain?"
- "Who should we clone next?"
- "What would Belsky and Thiel disagree about?"

### Patterns We Avoid

- "Introducing..." (boring, every launch post starts this way)
- "We're excited to announce..." (nobody cares about your excitement)
- "Imagine a world where..." (cliche)
- "What if I told you..." (LinkedIn cringe)
- Rhetorical questions that don't provoke ("Want to think better?" — obviously yes)
- Anything that starts with "In today's fast-paced world..." (kill it)

## 5. Messaging by Audience

### AI Power Users (Primary)

**Hook:** "Stop building context packs by hand."
**Value prop:** Pre-structured knowledge graphs with embeddings, connections, and 10 skills. Saves you hours of prompt curation.
**Proof point:** Show the JSON schema. Show the atom count. Show the install command.
**CTA:** `npx skills add brainsforsale/belsky`

### Ambitious Operators (Revenue driver)

**Hook:** "A $50K advisor for $29."
**Value prop:** Pressure-test your decisions against the actual thinking frameworks of proven leaders. Not generic AI advice — sourced, attributed, confidence-scored.
**Proof point:** Show a `/advise` output on a real business question. Side-by-side: Claude without brain vs. Claude with brain.
**CTA:** "Get this brain"

### Content Creators (Natural fit)

**Hook:** "Never run out of angles."
**Value prop:** 284 atomic ideas you can riff on, connect to your topic, and remix into original content. Attribution built in.
**Proof point:** Show `/surprise` → `/connect` → a LinkedIn post idea in 60 seconds.
**CTA:** "Get the brain. Get 284 post ideas."

### Thinker's Audience (Scale play)

**Hook:** "See how they really think."
**Value prop:** You've read every newsletter. Now see the connections, contradictions, and evolution you missed. The visual brain map is your new favorite thing.
**Proof point:** The explore.html visualization. Let them click around.
**CTA:** "Explore the brain"

### Builders / Developers (Premium tier)

**Hook:** "Wire expert knowledge into your app."
**Value prop:** Clean JSON, 1536-dim embeddings, typed connections. Plug into any RAG pipeline in under an hour.
**Proof point:** Show the schema. Show the embedding dimensions. Show a 5-line integration example.
**CTA:** "Get Pro" / "API docs"

## 6. Objection Responses

These are the responses that must show up on the landing page (via FAQ, inline copy, or demo sections).

| Objection | Our Response |
|-----------|-------------|
| "I can just ask Claude 'what would Belsky say'" | Claude guesses from training data. We give it 284 sourced atoms with connections and confidence scores. The output is fundamentally different. **Show the side-by-side.** |
| "I can build this myself" | You can. But will you? And will it have typed connections, confidence tiers, voice-enriched quotes, and 10 chained skills? Your time is worth more than $29. |
| "Context windows are huge now, I'll just paste the newsletter" | Raw text isn't structured knowledge. You lose connections, evolution tracking, contradiction detection, and the skill interface. Pasting text is like reading a dictionary instead of speaking a language. |
| "Is this ethical?" | Everything is sourced from public content with full attribution. If a thinker objects, their brain gets pulled immediately. We also offer "Claim Your Brain" — thinkers can verify, endorse, and share revenue. Ethics-first. |
| "Is 284 atoms enough?" | Belsky's brain covers all 77 editions of Implications at 100% coverage. Each atom is a discrete insight with source links, not a sentence fragment. And the connections between atoms multiply the value exponentially. |
| "Isn't this just book summaries?" | Summaries flatten. We structure. An atom isn't a summary — it's a discrete belief, framework, or principle with typed connections to other atoms, confidence scores, source dates, and original voice quotes. The graph is the product, not the text. |

## 8. Photography & Imagery

### What We Use

- **Product screenshots.** The product IS the visual. Terminal demos, explore.html visualizations, atom card layouts, skill outputs. The product sells itself visually.
- **Data visualization.** Knowledge graphs, connection maps, cluster visualizations. The brain's structure is inherently beautiful.
- **Typography as imagery.** Large display text, pull quotes from atoms, the install command as hero art. Words are our visuals.

### What We Don't Use

- Stock photography of any kind
- AI-generated "brain" imagery (glowing neurons, digital heads)
- Headshots of the thinkers (licensing issues, feels like endorsement)
- Abstract blobs, gradients-for-gradients'-sake, decorative illustrations
- Screenshots of other AI tools (ChatGPT interface, Claude UI) — our product is tool-agnostic

### The Explore.html as Brand Asset

The interactive visual brain (explore.html) IS our hero image. It's what people screenshot and share. Every brain ships with one. It should be prominently featured in all marketing.

## 9. Content Formats

### The Side-by-Side Demo (Most Important Marketing Asset)

Two columns:
- **Left:** "Claude without the brain" — generic, hedging, surface-level
- **Right:** "Claude with the Belsky brain" — sourced, specific, confidence-scored, with skill chaining suggestions

This answers objection #1 ("I can just ask Claude") and is worth more than any copy.

### The Brain Fight (Recurring Content Format)

One topic. Two brains. Head-to-head. "BRAIN FIGHT: Belsky vs. Galloway on whether brands will die." Works as:
- LinkedIn carousel
- X/Twitter thread
- Short-form video
- Newsletter feature
- Landing page social proof

### The Atom Drop (Micro-Content Engine)

Every brain has 100+ atoms. Each atom is a standalone social post. One atom = one LinkedIn slide, one X post, one newsletter snippet. CTA: "This is 1 of 284 atoms in the Belsky brain. Get the full brain for $29."

### This Week in Brains (Newsletter)

Weekly breakdown of one interesting insight from the catalog. Free content that drives paid purchases. Can be 100% generated from existing atoms.

## 10. Rules for AI-Generated Brand Content

When using AI to generate BrainsForSale marketing copy:

1. **Always show, don't tell.** If the AI writes "powerful" or "comprehensive," replace it with a specific number or a demo screenshot.
2. **Check for banned words.** Run generated copy against Section 4 ("Words We DON'T Use"). Kill anything that sounds like generic SaaS marketing.
3. **Test the "builder filter."** Would an indie developer or prompt engineer roll their eyes at this sentence? If yes, rewrite it.
4. **Maintain the three-beat cadence.** Short. Medium. Payoff. If a paragraph is longer than 3 sentences, break it up.
5. **Every piece of content needs a number.** Atom count, connection count, edition count, skill count, confidence score. Specifics are the brand.
6. **Attribution is non-negotiable.** Every claim about a thinker's views must be sourceable to atoms. No hallucinating positions they don't hold.
7. **The product IS the content.** Terminal demos, skill outputs, and explore.html screenshots should appear in every major piece of marketing. Don't describe the product — show it running.

---

## 11. Brand Don'ts (The Dealbreakers)

1. **Never imply the thinker endorsed the brain** unless they've formally claimed it.
2. **Never use "AI clone" or "digital twin"** — we structure knowledge, we don't clone people.
3. **Never promise the brain "thinks like" the person** — it surfaces their documented thinking. There's a difference.
4. **Never use generic AI visuals** (glowing brains, neural networks, robot hands).
5. **Never hide the data.** Show atom counts, brain scores, confidence tiers, source links. Transparency IS the brand.
6. **Never lock the product to one platform.** "Works with Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT, Gemini" is a first-paragraph statement, not a footnote.
7. **Never use warm/soft colors for CTAs.** Brain Indigo is the only CTA color. Knowledge Gold is decorative only.
8. **Never write body copy in Space Grotesk.** Display font is for headlines. Inter is for reading.
9. **Never round buttons into pills.** 8px radius. Pills are for badges and tags only.
10. **Never use pure black (`#000000`) for text on light backgrounds.** Always Deep Ink (`#0f172a`).
