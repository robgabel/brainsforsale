# BrainsForSale — Customer Personas

*Created: 2026-04-05. Source: brainstorm session with Rob.*

---

## How to Use This File

Each persona follows the same structure designed for maximum product decision utility:

- **JTBD** — The progress they're trying to make (situation → motivation → outcome)
- **Current workaround** — What they do today without brain packs
- **Trigger moment** — The specific event that sends them looking for a solution
- **Decision criteria** — What makes them choose BrainsForSale over alternatives
- **Objections** — The reason they almost don't buy (landing page + onboarding must address these)
- **Channels** — Where they hang out, who they trust, what content resonates
- **Willingness to pay** — Price sensitivity + budget source
- **Killer features** — Which of the 10 skills matter most to this persona
- **Success metric** — How THEY define "this was worth it"

**Priority stack rank:** Power Users (beta) > Ambitious Operators (revenue) > Thinker's Audience (scale) > Content Creators (natural fit) > Builders (premium) > Students (volume) > Team Leads (enterprise)

## 1. The AI Power User

**Archetype:** Indie builder, prompt engineer, AI-native knowledge worker
**Age range:** 25-45
**Size of market:** Small but growing fast. Tens of thousands today, millions in 2-3 years.

**JTBD:** "When I'm starting a new AI project or decision, I want pre-built expert thinking frameworks I can load instantly, so I can skip hours of context curation and get higher-quality output from my AI tools."

**Current workaround:** Hand-curating system prompts. Copy-pasting newsletter excerpts into Claude Projects. Building custom GPTs with manually assembled context. Bookmarking articles they'll never re-read.

**Trigger moment:** They just spent 45 minutes building a system prompt for a strategy session and realized they do this every time. Or they see someone else's Claude Project output that's clearly better because it has richer context.

**Decision criteria:**
- Quality of the knowledge structure (atoms + connections, not just text dumps)
- Portability (works in Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT — not locked to one platform)
- Time to value (how fast from install to useful output?)
- Data format (clean JSON, embeddings included?)

**Objections:**
- "I can build this myself" — Yes, but will you? And will it be as good?
- "Context windows are getting huge, I'll just paste the raw content" — Connections, evolution tracking, and skills are the value, not pre-chunking
- "Is 284 atoms enough to be useful?"

**Channels:** X/Twitter AI community, Hacker News, Claude Discord, indie hacker Slack groups, AI engineering newsletters (Latent Space, The Batch), GitHub trending

**Willingness to pay:** $29-49 personal credit card, no friction. Would pay $99+ for embeddings/API access. Price is not the blocker — proof of quality is.

**Killer features:** `/advise`, `/connect`, `/surprise`, raw JSON + embeddings for custom integrations

**Success metric:** "I loaded this brain and my AI session was noticeably better within 5 minutes." Speed to 'aha' moment.

## 2. The Ambitious Operator

**Archetype:** Solo founder, fractional exec, solopreneur scaling with AI. The "company of one" crowd.
**Age range:** 30-50
**Size of market:** Large and rapidly expanding. Justin Welsh has 800K+ followers; this is his audience.

**JTBD:** "When I'm making a high-stakes business decision alone, I want access to world-class strategic thinking I can pressure-test my ideas against, so I can make better decisions without a $50K advisor or co-founder."

**Current workaround:** Asking ChatGPT generic strategy questions. Reading books and newsletters but struggling to apply frameworks to their specific situation. Paying for coaching ($200-500/hr) or going without.

**Trigger moment:** They just made a decision they're not confident about — pricing, hiring, fundraising, product direction — and wished they had a sparring partner. Or they read a Belsky newsletter and thought "I wish I could ask him about MY business."

**Decision criteria:**
- Quality of advice output (does `/advise` actually feel like talking to a strategic thinker, not generic AI?)
- Relevance to their specific situation (does it adapt to their context?)
- Credibility of the thinker (would they read this person's book?)
- Speed (can they get useful input in under 2 minutes?)

**Objections:**
- "How is this better than just asking Claude 'what would Belsky say'?" — THIS IS THE #1 OBJECTION. The answer: structured atoms with connections and confidence scores produce fundamentally different output than Claude guessing from training data. Need a side-by-side demo.
- "I don't know who Scott Belsky is" — Brain selection matters. Need thinkers with name recognition in this audience.
- "$29/brain adds up if I want multiple" — Bundle pricing, or prove single-brain value first.

**Channels:** LinkedIn (Greg Isenberg, Justin Welsh, Sahil Bloom audiences), solopreneur newsletters, Indie Hackers, startup podcasts (My First Million, The Tim Ferriss Show), X/Twitter founder community

**Willingness to pay:** $29/brain is impulse purchase territory on personal card. Would pay $99/year for updates + multi-brain access. Company card if they have one.

**Killer features:** `/advise` (core), `/debate` (stress-test), `/apply` (make it actionable for their situation)

**Success metric:** "I used `/advise` on a real decision and it changed my thinking or gave me confidence in my direction." One great advisory session = hooked.

## 3. The Content Creator

**Archetype:** LinkedIn creator, newsletter writer, podcast host. Content is their job or their primary marketing channel.
**Age range:** 28-50
**Size of market:** Medium. Hundreds of thousands of active content creators on LinkedIn alone.

**JTBD:** "When I'm staring at a blank page needing a fresh angle, I want an inexhaustible source of interesting frameworks and contrarian takes to riff on, so I can publish consistently without running dry."

**Current workaround:** Reading 10+ newsletters for inspiration. Saving tweets and quotes to Notion. Recycling their own past frameworks. Asking AI for "give me a hot take on X" (which produces generic output).

**Trigger moment:** They've posted 3 times this week and every idea feels stale. Or they see a competitor's post that references a framework they've never heard of, and it's getting 10x their engagement.

**Decision criteria:**
- Volume and variety of ideas (are there enough atoms to fuel weeks of content?)
- Uniqueness (will this give them angles nobody else is posting?)
- Attribution clarity (can they credit properly without it feeling like plagiarism?)
- Cross-brain mashups (novel combinations = original content)

**Objections:**
- "Isn't this just repackaging someone else's ideas?" — The skill is in connecting, applying, and remixing. Attribution is built into every output.
- "I need to sound like ME, not Belsky" — The brain provides frameworks; your voice applies them. `/connect` bridges brain ideas to YOUR topic.
- "What if everyone buys the same brain and we all post the same frameworks?" — 284 atoms x your unique context = effectively infinite unique outputs.

**Channels:** LinkedIn (Rob's own channel), creator economy communities, ConvertKit/Beehiiv creator networks, content strategy podcasts, "how I write" threads on X

**Willingness to pay:** $29-49 per brain, personal card. Multi-brain bundles at $79-99 are the play here — mashups are the unique value. Would expense to business if they monetize content.

**Killer features:** `/surprise` (daily inspiration), `/connect` (bridge to their topic), `/mashup` (cross-brain = unique angles), `/brainfight` (debate content)

**Success metric:** "I got 3 post ideas in 10 minutes that I wouldn't have come up with on my own." Volume of usable ideas per session.

## 4. The Student / Lifelong Learner

**Archetype:** MBA student, self-directed learner, ambitious 20-something building their mental model library. Farnam Street readers.
**Age range:** 20-35
**Size of market:** Large. Millions of people buy books, courses, and summaries. Blinkist has 30M+ users.

**JTBD:** "When I'm trying to deeply understand how a great thinker reasons, I want to explore their ideas as an interconnected system — not just summaries — so I can internalize their mental models and apply them to my own decisions."

**Current workaround:** Reading books (slow, linear). Using Blinkist/Shortform summaries (shallow). Watching YouTube explainers. Taking notes in Notion that they never revisit. Highlighting Kindle passages that disappear into the void.

**Trigger moment:** They just finished a book and realized they can't actually recall or apply any of it two weeks later. Or they're in an MBA class discussing frameworks and wish they could query the original thinker's full body of work.

**Decision criteria:**
- Depth (is this deeper than a book summary?)
- Interactivity (can I ask questions, not just read?)
- Intellectual credibility (is the source material respected?)
- Learning progression (does it help me build understanding over time?)

**Objections:**
- "$29 is a lot when I can read the blog for free" — The blog is linear; the brain is a queryable graph with connections, contradictions, and evolution. Different product.
- "Is this actually educational or just a novelty?" — `/teach` + `/evolve` + `/deep-dive` create genuine learning workflows, not just lookup.
- "I don't use Claude/Cursor" — Needs a zero-setup option (explore.html in browser, or hosted web UI in v2).

**Channels:** Farnam Street audience, Ali Abdaal / Thomas Frank audiences, book summary app users, university AI clubs, MBA Slack groups, Reddit (r/productivity, r/selfimprovement, r/artificial)

**Willingness to pay:** Price-sensitive. $14.99 might be the sweet spot for students. Free sample brain (Einstein, public domain figure) is the gateway. Parents or university might fund. Gift purchases are a real channel here.

**Killer features:** `/teach` (explain like this thinker would), `/evolve` (how thinking changed), `/deep-dive` (full topic exploration), explore.html (visual unboxing)

**Success metric:** "I understand this person's thinking well enough to explain it to someone else and apply it to a new situation." Retained understanding, not just exposure.

## 5. The Builder / Developer

**Archetype:** Developer building AI-powered products who needs high-quality structured knowledge for RAG, fine-tuning, or specialized agents.
**Age range:** 25-40
**Size of market:** Growing fast. Every AI startup needs domain knowledge; few have good sources.

**JTBD:** "When I'm building an AI product that needs domain expertise, I want a clean, well-structured knowledge graph with embeddings and typed connections, so I can wire expert-level knowledge into my app without months of curation work."

**Current workaround:** Scraping blogs and podcasts. Manual curation into vector databases. Using Wikipedia or generic datasets. Hiring domain experts to create training data ($$$).

**Trigger moment:** They're building a product advisor, research assistant, or domain-specific chatbot and the knowledge layer is garbage — generic, unstructured, no connections. They realize curation is 80% of the work and they hate it.

**Decision criteria:**
- Data quality (clean schema, consistent structure, typed connections)
- Embedding format (1536-dim, compatible with their vector DB)
- Licensing (can they use it commercially?)
- Coverage (how complete is the knowledge graph?)

**Objections:**
- "I can scrape and embed this myself" — You can, but the curation, connection typing, confidence scoring, and voice extraction are the hard parts. Raw embeddings of blog posts are not the same thing.
- "Will this work with my stack?" — JSON + standard embeddings + no platform lock-in. It's designed for this.
- "One thinker's knowledge isn't enough for my product" — Multi-brain bundles. Or use it as a template for building your own domain brains.

**Channels:** GitHub (readme + stars), Hacker News, LangChain/LlamaIndex communities, AI engineering newsletters, Dev.to, Discord servers for AI builders

**Willingness to pay:** $99-199 for the data pack with embeddings. Would pay $299+ for API access with semantic search. Company card / startup budget. Price anchored against the cost of building it themselves (weeks of eng time).

**Killer features:** Raw JSON data, pre-computed embeddings, typed connection graph, clean schema. Skills are nice-to-have but the data artifact is the product for this persona.

**Success metric:** "I wired this into my RAG pipeline in under an hour and the output quality jumped immediately." Integration speed + quality delta.

## 6. The Thinker's Audience / True Fans

**Archetype:** Devoted follower of the specific thinker. Subscribes to their newsletter, reads every post, buys every book. Wants to go deeper.
**Age range:** 25-55 (varies by thinker)
**Size of market:** Per-brain. Belsky's Implications: ~50K subscribers. Attia's Drive: millions of listeners. Each brain unlocks a pre-existing audience.

**JTBD:** "When I've read everything this thinker has published and want to explore their ideas at a deeper level, I want to see how their ideas connect, contradict, and evolve, so I can understand not just WHAT they think but HOW they think."

**Current workaround:** Re-reading old posts. Searching their newsletter archive manually. Making their own notes and connections. Following the thinker on every platform hoping for new content.

**Trigger moment:** They just read a new post and thought "didn't they say something different about this 2 years ago?" Or they're in a debate and want to cite the thinker's actual position with evidence.

**Decision criteria:**
- Completeness (does it cover everything, not just highlights?)
- Accuracy (does it faithfully represent the thinker's views, including nuance and evolution?)
- The thinker's endorsement (if claimed, this is a massive trust signal)
- The explore.html experience (the visual knowledge graph IS the product for this persona)

**Objections:**
- "Is this officially endorsed?" — Might not be (yet). Transparency about sourcing matters. The "claim your brain" feature flips this from risk to opportunity.
- "I already know this person's work" — You know it linearly. The graph shows you connections and contradictions you've never noticed. `/evolve` reveals intellectual shifts you missed.
- "Is this ethically okay?" — Fair use of public content, with full attribution. But if the thinker objects, their brain gets pulled. Ethics-first positioning.

**Channels:** The thinker's own newsletter/community (THE distribution channel), social shares of the explore.html visualization, book launch tie-ins, conference appearances

**Willingness to pay:** $29-49 enthusiastically. They buy the thinker's books, courses, and merch. This is cheaper than most alternatives. Gift purchases to other fans.

**Killer features:** explore.html (visual brain), `/evolve` (how thinking changed), `/deep-dive` (full topic lens), `/brainfight` (vs. another thinker they follow)

**Success metric:** "I discovered a connection or contradiction in their thinking I'd never noticed despite reading them for years." The 'aha' of seeing familiar ideas as a network.

## 7. The Team Lead / Culture Builder

**Archetype:** VP, director, team lead who wants to align their team around a shared thinking framework. Building culture through shared mental models.
**Age range:** 35-50
**Size of market:** Large enterprise-adjacent market, but long sales cycle. Every management team that does book clubs or offsite workshops.

**JTBD:** "When I want my team to make better decisions using proven frameworks, I want a shared thinking tool we can all reference, so we develop a common vocabulary and decision-making approach without expensive workshops or consultants."

**Current workaround:** Team book clubs (nobody finishes the book). $10K-50K offsite workshops. Sharing articles in Slack that nobody reads. Hiring consultants to install frameworks.

**Trigger moment:** Their team just made a bad decision that a known framework would have caught. Or they came back from a conference inspired by a speaker and want to operationalize the ideas. Or they're scaling fast and need decision-making consistency across new hires.

**Decision criteria:**
- Ease of team deployment (can they set it up for 10 people in an afternoon?)
- Quality of shared experience (does the team actually use it, or does it collect dust?)
- ROI story for their manager (how do they justify the expense?)
- Integration with team tools (Claude for Work, Cursor team, etc.)

**Objections:**
- "My team won't use another tool" — It lives inside the tools they already use (Claude, Cursor). Zero new apps.
- "Hard to measure ROI on 'better thinking'" — Track usage: how many `/advise` calls per week? Decision quality is hard to measure, but adoption is not.
- "We'd need approval / procurement" — Team pricing needs to be simple and expensable without a procurement cycle. $199 for 10 seats is under most approval thresholds.

**Channels:** Management newsletters, L&D conferences, LinkedIn (targeting VPs of Product, Strategy, Ops), team productivity tools (Notion, Confluence integrations), enterprise Slack/Teams directories

**Willingness to pay:** $99-199 for team license (10 seats) on company card. L&D budget. Would pay $499+ for workshop-style onboarding. Price anchored against consulting ($10K+) and conferences ($5K+).

**Killer features:** Shared brain loaded in team workspace, `/advise` for group decisions, `/debate` for strategy meetings, `/teach` for onboarding new hires to the team's thinking framework

**Success metric:** "My team references this brain's frameworks in actual meetings and decision docs without me prompting them." Organic adoption as shared language.

## Cross-Persona Insights

**The side-by-side demo is everything.** Personas 1, 2, and 3 all need to see: "Here's what Claude says without the brain. Here's what it says WITH the brain." If the delta isn't obvious in 30 seconds, the product fails.

**Multi-brain is the retention engine.** Single-brain buyers churn. Multi-brain buyers (personas 3, 6, 7) stick because mashups and brainfights create unique value that compounds. Pricing should incentivize the second brain purchase.

**The thinker IS the distribution.** Every new brain unlocks a built-in audience (persona 6). Brain selection is a marketing decision as much as a product one. Prioritize thinkers with large, engaged, digitally-native audiences.

**Free sample brain is non-negotiable for persona 4.** Students won't pay $29 on faith. Einstein, Feynman, or another public domain figure with rich source material. This also serves as the demo for all other personas.
