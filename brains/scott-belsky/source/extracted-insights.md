## Superhumanity & Human Advantage

**Taste as human moat in AI world.** Three drivers: Inputs (experiences and knowledge you seek), Filters (what you actively block out to preserve uniqueness of your lens), Discernment (choices you make). As AI commoditizes making things, value shifts to those with superior ability to choose. Taste is noticing what is relevant before the algorithm does. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/what-are-we-gonna-do-now-winning))

**Two possible futures: humans less important vs. more important.** Superhumanity is where humans become tastemakers and contrarians by default, optimize for experiences over productivity, override ancestral fears, and become exponentially more audacious. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/what-are-we-gonna-do-now-winning))

**Agency as human superpower.** Naive belief that anything is possible despite odds. AI makes calculated decisions based on statistics. Humans possess miraculous potential to believe they are capable of anything, if only out of ignorance. Human ignorance and audacity to defy odds has changed the world countless times. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/what-are-we-gonna-do-now-winning))

**AI as jazz partner.** Engage AI with flexibility, not fully formed plans. Discover instruments AI is best at, complement it with what it lacks: taste, agency, natural human tendencies. Force-quit ancestral conformity programs running in our heads to free cognitive RAM for developing taste and sharpening discernment. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/what-are-we-gonna-do-now-winning))

**AI as giant cushion.** Should try far more audacious things when 90% of obstacles disappear. Technology enables learning any expertise or capability as needed. This sensation of superhuman capability and self-sufficiency is agency. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/what-are-we-gonna-do-now-winning))

## Originality & Craft

**Remaking a masterpiece is not hard; making a masterpiece is hard.** As AI enables infinite riffs and rips of existing IP, originality becomes the primary ingredient of timeless creations. Culture will favor originality as the market floods with familiar slop. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**Originality and quality carry a premium.** Consumers overwhelmed with copycat slop crave MORE original high-quality storytelling. Like fast food did not threaten fine cuisine. Modern media brands will play a Michelin-star role curating quality. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**Hollywood craft vs. AI slop divergence.** Stark difference between content creators who trade control for speed and artists who preserve control and precision. Prompt-based slop tools focus on social/creator use cases while craft-preserving technology gets lasting traction. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

**Behind-the-scenes "proof of craft" enters mainstream.** As AI content fills feeds, a membrane of doubt develops. Only when we see ingenuity and craftsmanship behind content are we mesmerized. Process becomes part of the end product. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

**Origin stories and flaws as evidence of authenticity.** Art gains value through story and provenance. As we seek originality we will look for humanity. Deliberate artifacts of humanity including flaws are a subtle flex of the people and struggle behind the work. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**Taste as pursuit of originality.** Like the pursuit of health requires a nutritious diet, the pursuit of taste requires consumption of original ideas and stories. Identity formation will favor unique experiences, wildly original content, and eccentricity. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**Scarcity and craft thrive in age of abundance.** When anything becomes ubiquitous it drives human desire for scarcity, craft, and meaning. As shoes are commoditized we buy higher-end shoes. Expect more limited editions, artist-personalized apparel, stories behind objects, and creative "gates" to purchase exclusive items. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

## Agentic Commerce & Brands

**Three waves of agentic commerce.** Wave 1 (now): Integrated and reactive -- shopping suggestions in LLM chats via Universal Commerce Protocol (Shopify + Google). Wave 2: Integrated and proactive -- AI starts conversations based on your data. Wave 3: Autonomous -- smart sensors automate commerce, agents authorized to purchase. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Word of Agent (WOA) marketing replaces word of mouth.** Product stack decisions increasingly proposed and set up by AI agents (Claude Code recommending Supabase, Railway, Modal). Companies need MCPs and agent-friendly gateways to become the preferred option for agents. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Brand relevance in agentic commerce is a paradox.** AI knows us better than we know ourselves so we take suggestions by default (brand matters less). But commoditized interfaces may drive seeking brands for trust and flex (brand matters more). Brands that build category trust or serve as identity flex will thrive. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Agent negotiation and personalized pricing.** Commerce agents inform multiple merchants of purchase intent, allow bidding. Pricing personalized by loyalty, taste, social influence, and viral coefficient. Discriminatory pricing concerns emerge. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Goodbye intermediaries.** Travel agents, real estate brokers, insurance brokers replaced by trusted AI agents. We overestimated value of human relationships and endured unnecessary friction and fees for a human face. Massive disruption of the intermediation layer. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Social recommendations still powerful.** Humans trust one friend's testimonial over thousands of stranger ratings. LLMs need social graphs to enable this. Surprising that no major LLM besides Meta has a comprehensive social graph. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Hospitality differentiates commerce.** Technology replaces humans behind the scenes; more humans deployed front of house. Stores and websites feel like hotels where preferences are known. Agents handle payment so human interaction focuses on craft and human touches. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

## AI Agents & Network Effects

**Agent-only networks are the next chapter of AI.** Connecting agents powered by different LLMs with different data sources to help and learn from one another. Having an agent work alone becomes antiquated. Real opportunity is network effect where more diverse agents = better outcomes. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

**Agent networks will add commerce.** Specialized agents with differentiated data access can be paid by other agents. Next cycle for Bitcoin and permissionless value exchange may be powered by AI agent networking since agents don't have IDs for KYC. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

## Software, Code & Moats

**Exponentially more code.** Generation of code no longer constrained by human hours. Abundance attacks long-tail problems, enables bespoke solutions, personalized software, and end-to-end agentic workflows made and iterated by AI agents. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

**Disposable software.** The app equivalent of the microsite. Software generated to solve temporary problems, then discarded. Vastly increases value of deployment, management, and observability tools. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

**Moats of graphs, security, coordination, and shared memory are underestimated.** More code created means these underlying services become more important. If your service is source of truth, achieves the outcome, or enables agents to function, you're in a good position. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

**Ordinary data becomes less valuable moat.** Connectors make data ubiquitous. New proprietary data moats: graphs (who knows who), portable personalization, and real-time data sources increasingly captured by robots. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

**Rise of internal dev teams replacing SaaS.** Companies vibe-code tailor-made software. Initially replace expensive PE-owned clunky SaaS, then homegrown tools collapse functions into one another (legal+finance, social+sales+support). ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

**Pricing models must evolve beyond seats.** Bigger opportunity in variable pricing per task or unit of labor, and outcome-based pricing where agents earn their keep like a salesperson. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

**Hardware becomes a moat.** When everyone can make software, unique products tightly couple hardware and software (Whoop, Oura, Board, Meter). New picks-and-shovels companies streamline chip design and supply chain. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

**Power in consumer AI shifts to hardware + OS providers.** Open-source models running locally plus capable consumer hardware creates massive shift. Implications for chips, OS evolution, and the role of open source. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

## Organizational Design & Leadership

**Lack of change management is the ultimate advantage.** During platform shifts, wisdom is evenly distributed but execution takes years. Change management is human rewiring, not technical. Teams without that burden gain most advantage. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

**Revenue per employee as future metric.** Moving from bragging about company size to how small it is. As ROI per employee rises, companies deploy MORE people to launch NEW products rather than scale existing ones. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**Everyone must own a metric end-to-end.** AI development unleashes direct ownership, impact, and accountability. No more pointing fingers at dependencies. Middle managers and alignment processes replaced by direct metric ownership. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**IT becomes the most important team.** Fastest growing teams will deploy and monitor agentic tools, oversee permissions and security, and build tailor-made software. Currently these centralized teams don't exist or aren't sufficiently empowered. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-premium-of-originality-revenue))

**Port people and products to prevent fiefdom building.** Proactively transfer great people to bigger roles even when current work is thriving. Like re-potting a fast-growing tree for deeper roots. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Promote unpopular people.** Controversial people who took risks get punished for past shortcomings. Leaders ahead of their time get punished. Celebrate bold bets and lessons learned over safe track records. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Celebrate impact not tenure.** Celebrating years at company sends clear message to new talent that we value longevity over impact. Celebrate shipping, risk-taking, clearing nonsense, delighting customers. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Be willing to be misunderstood before results materialize.** Most impactful big-company decisions delay near-term for long-term. Major transformative projects rarely ship on time but need to be done anyway. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Fight the soundbites.** Executives make snap thin-slice judgments. Ground yourself in data and principles. Poke at metrics and time periods when people say something "didn't work." ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Apprenticeships are the new entry-level jobs.** As AI automates tasks once done by inexperienced employees, knowledge transfer is at risk. Companies will launch shadow programs. The apprenticeship model was the greatest casualty of the industrial revolution and is overdue for a comeback. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/exponential-code-network-effects))

## Memory & Ambient AI

**Malleable memory is a feature not a bug.** We survive by annotating and rationalizing memories. But persistent ambient devices and AI summarization will make boundless memory a choice. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**New digital addiction: living in the past.** When memories become immersive video automatically captured by ambient devices, the temptation to relive the past becomes irresistible. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Memory deletion and augmentation as a service.** New cultural practices of deleting digital artifacts from certain life periods. AI tools to remove trauma-associated memories or edit memories to be more favorable. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Instagram filter for memories.** Most successful consumer use-cases of memory tech will make our past feel more interesting. AI-edited memories that inspire and embolden us. Over time, edited digital memories influence our biological ones. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Who inherits our memories?** For 300,000+ years humanity benefited from new generations feeling independent. Death of previous generation is ultimate forcing function for independence. Should we proactively delete ourselves when the time comes? ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Lived-span: the life you feel you have lived.** Beyond lifespan, healthspan, and joyspan. Experiences with texture (surprise, hardship) create core memories. The more we remember, the more we feel we have lived. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/implications-of-remembering-everything))

**Ambient listening goes mainstream.** Local models process daily life, discard recordings, yield self-awareness and instant recall. Implications for catching biases, new conversational punctuation, sharing memory. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/12-outlooks-for-the-future-2026))

## Consumer AI & Product Ideas

**Consumer AI is the missing topic.** Beyond skeuomorphic chat-with-LLM products, how might AI unleash entirely different social apps and consumer experiences? Our future can't be limited to endless chats. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Peanut Gallery.** Social app where you post media and get responses from 100 AI characters with specific backstories. Only AI can comment. Humans are spectators. Mechanics for trialing posts before sharing with humans. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Social simulations with digital twins.** Deploy digital twins into simulated social scenarios. Rehearse conversations, predict outcomes. Will it be irresponsible to make decisions WITHOUT running a simulation first? ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**AI companions with deep memory.** Products like Tolan designed to help users feel grounded and connected. Engagement patterns in the AI friend space show surprising depth. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**AI dating and wingman-as-a-service.** Shift from high-frequency low-vetted to lower-frequency highly-informed matches. Agent-to-agent pre-meeting before human introduction. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

**Unsaid reasons for product usage are critical.** People are not rational. Accounting product got more usage when users could put their name on exports (credit). We log into Instagram after posting to see who liked us (ego). Technology optimizing for efficiency must recognize these irrational motivations. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/where-is-consumer-ai-unsaid-reasons))

## Scaling Without Growing & The Meaning Economy

**Small teams get the advantages of the big.** AI-native tools no longer require growing headcount to scale ambition. Every function of an organization will be refactored to allow small teams to scale reach without proportionate headcount growth. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**Intent-driven AI replaces functional tool stacks.** Instead of buying a sales tool and hiring proportionate sales people, companies will engage specialized AI oriented around intended outcomes — paying for work completed or results rather than seats. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**The STBB (Small Team, Big Business) wave.** Massive growth in small businesses with millions in revenue and healthy margins. AI tools yield a barbell outcome: massive small business growth plus trillion-dollar AI behemoths, with the middle getting crushed. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**Consumer demand for artisanal will grow in an AI world.** As big companies flood attention with cheaper personalized everything, consumers will crave more scarce and authentically human experiences — often provided by passionate small businesses. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**The "meaning economy" supplants the creator economy.** In a world of content abundance and zero-cost creation, meaning — the story, purpose, myths behind creators, and brand value — commands a premium over content itself. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**The art of business is what doesn't scale.** The science of business is scaling, but the art — curation, taste development, human storytelling — becomes increasingly critical as artists freed by AI have more time to explore ideas. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**Adaptability > Strategy during platform shifts.** Playbooks cannot be sacred cows — they expire quickly at the edge of what's next. Sunk cost fallacy is fatal during Cambrian explosions. Distinguish between technical challenges (change your technology) and adaptive challenges (YOU have to change). ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**Company distrust is the default in the data era.** Companies that store customer content and data need to proactively state what they will and won't do with those licenses. Transparency is now a product requirement. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**Spelling mistakes as artifacts of humanity.** In a world of AI-generated content, human imperfections become refreshing signals of authenticity. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

**Workflow converges with flow.** AI removes friction and allows people to express whatever is in their mind's eye without constraints — reaching creative flow faster and staying in it longer. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-scaling-without-growing))

## Talent Density, Feeling Special & Moving Past Prompts

**Talent density goes up.** AI-assisted management tools and offloading of mundane work to agents means the people empowered in each function will be more qualified generalists or have deep differentiating superpowers, replacing mediocre middle managers. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**DeFi is the payments layer for AI agents.** Credit cards weren't designed for instant autonomous micropayments between agents — but crypto/DeFi infrastructure was built for exactly this kind of frictionless machine-to-machine transaction. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**AI transforms builders more than consumers (so far).** Builders get transformative tools (natural-language network management, code-generating agents, AI-powered 3D illustration), while consumers have gotten incrementally better search and editing — not yet life-transforming. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**Consumer AI breakthrough = personalization + private data.** Apps that privately collect and manage personal data — health, fitness, calendar, locations — and surface insights will be the killer consumer AI use case. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**"Feeling special, as a service."** AI enables luxury-grade personalized experiences at scale. Every retailer knowing your shoe size, every restaurant knowing your allergies, every product knowing your skill level during onboarding. The winning consumer experiences make people feel uniquely catered to. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**The "controls era" of creativity replaces prompts.** Controls allow sophisticated, specific modifications — unlocking 100x terrain of possibility, much as Photoshop once did for physical paint brushes. The prompt era was merely a distraction. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**Creative risk unlocked by AI.** "Creative risk" = the wild ideas we don't pursue because cost is too much to bear. AI dramatically reduces the cost to explore and test far edges of imagination, building conviction in bold ideas once too risky. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**Delaying gratification wins.** Sticking together long enough to figure it out is one of the greatest competitive advantages. An incredible culture and genuine love for the topic are the ultimate hacks. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**Optimistic about the future, pessimistic about the present.** Great leaders exude confidence about potential paired with concern about today's progress. Founders should own what they haven't figured out yet — don't promote, engage with hard truths. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

**Get customers to talk about their problems, not your product.** Seek to understand frustrations rather than showing prototypes and asking "do you like this?" Nothing inhibits innovation more than optimizing for familiarity. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/talent-density-feeling-special-as))

## The Cognition Stack & Cognicos

**Every company becomes a "cognico."** The next-generation company runs on inference engines leveraging pre-trained AI models and proprietary data. Organizational functions become "nodes" rather than departments — designed for cognition, not helping people work together. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**The nucleus is compute and data.** Once pre-trained models reach their limits, the game shifts to the inference/reasoning layer — real-time computation leveraging company-specific data and logic. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**Humans as stewards, orchestrators, and leaders.** Stewards ensure model quality and data integrity. Orchestrators conduct logic across AI components with great taste. Leaders set goals, maintain humanity, and advocate for non-scalable human activities. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**"Orchestration designers" are the new product leaders.** They understand AI model capabilities, work with visual orchestration tools, have great taste, and are the final mile of human selection that differentiates companies. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**Rules engines become indispensable.** APIs governing autonomous agent behavior — for laws, regulations, brand compliance, pricing — will be a critical layer of the cognition stack. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**Value accrues to the first cognicos in each industry.** Too much capital may be going to companies making AI models while the majority of value accrues to the first to expertly use them in each industry. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**Social + support + sales = one channel.** Every support moment is a sales moment, every social complaint is a marketing moment. New organizational designs required for these collapsed functions. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**Seat-based → outcome-based business models.** Companies will pay for "cooked compute," outcomes, performance, and expertise by project — not seats. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

**Demand for scarce, authentic, offline experiences explodes.** Small restaurants, one-of-a-kind art, theater, emotional films, live music. 10-100x more small efficient companies will emerge serving niche human-crafted demands. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-cognition-stack-for-ai-native))

## Shared Memory & Knowledge Arbitrage

**Individual memory becomes collective.** Tools like Glean, Notion, and Granola make everything done or saved by individuals enrich the collective organizational knowledge available to everyone. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**AI memory becomes shareable and inheritable.** Consumer AI memory will allow sharing selective access with family and loved ones. Your accumulated knowledge of restaurants, health concerns, and preferences becomes an asset others can leverage — and inherit. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**Shared memory creates new power dynamics.** In the enterprise: who gets access to collective memory? When you leave a job, does the company keep your memory? When you hire or marry someone, they share your context window. Trust becomes paramount. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**We're in a "knowledge arbitrage" moment.** Young AI-native talent who use LLMs reflexively can capitalize on the fact that corporate leaders don't understand these tools — just as social-native experts did during the 2007-2010 social media shift. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**AI tutoring = universal 1:1 education.** A personalized tutor produces "two sigma improvement" in learning achievement. AI makes this universally accessible at near-zero cost. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**Tutor for life, tailor-made for you.** Extended context windows covering every aspect of your learning style, struggles, interests, and strengths. Traditional education's failure to accommodate different learning styles will seem ridiculous in retrospect. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**AI expands the market for education.** Personal AI tutors will take us down meandering paths of discovery, potentially antiquating "grade levels" and enabling "vibe careers" through lateral industry moves later in life. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**The greatest impact of AI in education is confidence.** When software meets you where you are and taps into what makes you special, we become vastly more capable and confident. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**100x more cycles of ideation.** Writers debate character mindset with LLMs, filmmakers pre-visualize scenes — shifting creative teams from production to exploring more ideas and polishing the final mile. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

**Cheaper creative exploration = more creative risk.** If new tools help creators visualize and experiment at a fraction of the cost, studios can advance bold new ideas instead of relying on safe franchises and sequels. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/shared-memory-knowledge-arbitrage))

## Summarized Living, Product Flex & AR

**The Era of Summarized Living.** Devices like Plaud and software like Granola make every discussion part of a permanent intelligent memory that summarizes and analyzes rather than storing verbatim recordings. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))

**Summarized living removes bias.** Our natural memories are terrible and biased. Factual AI summaries bring more accountability, meritocracy, and help us recognize our own destructive tendencies. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))

**Summaries may be legally safer than verbatim records.** In a litigious world, summaries with interpretation rather than attributable quotes could protect the innocent from misconstrued statements. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))

**We'll converse differently for summarization.** People will add trigger words for search, be careful about attribution, and become more fact-grounded given AI post-meeting analysis. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))

**Products succeed by enabling flex.** Five types: creative flex (originality), skills flex (leaderboards), wit flex (humor = intelligence), cultural flex (IYKYK), and social/status flex (envy). The "ego analytics" of a product drive engagement and retention. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))

**The kiss of death is trying too hard.** Successful products make flexing seem passive — something that just happened. Snapchat's Snap Map enables cultural and status flex while claiming to just be a map. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))

**AR is the next platform shift hiding in plain sight.** AR vibes today mirror AI vibes in 2016 — feels far away, startups too early, investors skeptical. But the ultimate interface will be an inch from our retinas. ([source](https://www.implications.com/p/the-era-of-summarized-living-what))
